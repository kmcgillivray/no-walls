No Walls

The United States is facing a new challenge to our identity. This is because Donald Trump was elected to the Presidency—in part—by appealing to instincts and fears that bring out the worst in us. His campaign was held aloft with pointed fingers, and we saw many or our friends and neighbors flock to Trump with their backs pressed together and with anger on their lips. In this election, we have seen how easy it is for us to lose sight of what is truly great about America. But most heartbreaking of all is the idea that his rise to our nation’s highest office has been a tacit enshrinement of the fear and anger he used to buoy himself to victory.

We cannot let those emotions define who we are as Americans. In the next few years, it is going to be critical for us to remind one another of what we have always known: our strength is our diversity, our greatness is our empathy, and our nation has always been—and always will be—one of open arms and open hearts. We must resist the urge to put up barriers between ourselves and others, because whether it is our Muslim friends, our LGBTQ neighbors, our African-American families, or our immigrant and multicultural communities, we must always see them as a precious and irreplaceable piece of the diverse fabric that makes America truly great.

The United States of America needs no walls.

—Joshua Atkins, English Teacher, Grafton, WI
